About This Tool

In today's digital world, personal data is constantly collected and stored by websites, apps, and third parties. Many people unknowingly expose sensitive information, increasing the risk of identity theft, fraud, or data breaches. This tool helps you become more aware of your data privacy risks and take steps to protect your personal information. This tool helps you understand the risks associated with sharing personal data online. By selecting the types of data you have shared, our system evaluates your exposure level and provides a risk assessment based on vulnerability and data significance.

The importance of data in the 21st century is often overlooked. The Data Brokering Industry or Data brokers collect and gather many types of personal data to sell to third parties often without the individuals' consent. Data broker companies like Acxiom and Equifax have collected a large database full of personal data and the industry as a whole is estimated to be worth USD 471.25 Billion by 2032. Beauvisage et al. (2020). Governments worldwide have increasingly sought access to personal data for various administrative, regulatory, and security purposes. F. Cate et al. (2012). With the expansion of digital space, the government has taken more importance on individuals' activities and personal data to improve and develop a better administration and security capabilities. Personal data is essential in academic research as well, particularly in fields like biomedical research, where data from millions worldwide is analyzed to improve health outcomes. The digitalization of health data, aided by social media and devices like smartwatches, has increased the scope of research but also intensified concerns about privacy and secondary data use. Parimi et al. (2023).
